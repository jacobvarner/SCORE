S.C.O.R.E. Holiday Gift Guide 2016

Ozobot 2.0 Bit Starter Pack ($60) / Ozobot Evo Starter Pack ($90)

These byte-size robots are a great gateway into the world of robotics for children 6 and up (although we have seen children even younger have fun with them). They use optical sensors to follow lines and to respond to commands. These can be programmed with or without computers using a version of Blockly (developed by Google!) or marker color codes.

The Evo version adds sounds, remote control mode (on iOS and Android), and obstacle-avoiding sensors!


Dash ($125) / Dot ($50) / Wonder Pack ($230)

It seems like these little guys are on everyone’s gift guides and it’s easy to see why. Aimed at elementary aged students, these cute robots are a non-intimidating way to get started programming. Dash can respond to voice, dance around, and sing! It has onboard sensors to do obstacle avoidance and your child can program new behaviors or record new sounds.

Dot is the brains of a robot and can be programmed to play games or even make your own games.

Dash can move around on its own but Dot cannot. If you have a hard time deciding, go with the Wonder Pack which gives you one of each robot and accessories which include a ball launcher, xylophone, building brick connectors, and more.


CoDrone ($180)

This cool little drone is fully-programmable in the Arduino (see below) programming environment which means that your student-engineer will learn using a real-world programming language. Recommended for advanced middle-school students and up, CoDrone has detailed tutorials and Robolink even hosts a competition using these drones. You can pick up two of them and even program them to do laser aerial battles!


Sphero SPRK+ ($130) / Sphero Star Wars BB-8 ($130/$199 w/ Force Band)

These neat little sphere-shaped robots can be programmed, remote-controlled, and can even go swimming. The BB-8 version above is Star Wars themed and you can even use the Force to control it. Play through missions on both versions to unlock new features (such as pre-programmed dances).


Parrot Mambo ($100) / Parrot Cargo Mini Drone ($64)

We used the Parrot Cargo Mini Drones for our first-ever Drone Camp this past summer with students in 7th-9th grade (although adults and younger students have had plenty of fun with these as well). They can be programmed or flown with a phone or tablet or you can get the optional controller as well. They are extremely stable, easy to fly, and are small enough that they don’t require FAA registration. Go ahead and pick up an extra battery and spare propellers. Trust me, you’ll want to have them around.

The update Mambo includes a ball-shooter and grabber accessory for even more drone fun.


Arduino Starter Kit ($85) / Raspberry Pi Starter Kit ($75)

These are gifts for the true nerds in your life. These are very similar devices but have slightly different applications. They both allow inputs and outputs (sensors and motors!) but Arduino boards are meant to do simple, repeated tasks (automated gardening, motion-controlled night lights, or custom light-shows). Raspberry Pi is a tiny computer for more advanced tasks such as a retro-arcade console, computer vision (not for the faint of heart), or even build your own laptop!

* All prices are accurate at the time of creating this list, but may change throughout the holiday season
